Efficient Transformers for real time EEG inference

Transformer models are attractive for EEG because they can model long temporal dependencies without
hand built features, but vanilla self attention is a poor fit for edge BCI settings. For a sequence of length  ,
full self attention forms an  ×  attention map, so both compute and memory scale as ( 2). In EEG
motor imagery, where multi channel signals are sampled densely over windows of several seconds, this
cost quickly becomes the main barrier to low latency inference. The literature in this project converges
on a simple response: constrain attention to local neighborhoods, build hierarchy across windows, or
replace pairwise attention with sparse or projected variants so the effective cost grows only linearly or
near linearly with sequence length [Bel20, Wan20, Wu21, Liu21b].

In the EEG literature, the strongest evidence does not yet come from one single linear attention family.
Instead, it comes from three partially overlapping design lines. The first is windowed and hierarchical
attention for MI decoding, where temporal neighborhoods are processed in fixed or shifted windows and
then fused across scales [Mou24, Wan23, Hua23b, Den23]. The second is compact hybrid backbones,
where convolution reduces token count and injects local inductive bias before a small Transformer block
models longer range structure [Son22, Keu24, Asa25]. The third is hardware minded deployment work,
where models are explicitly sized for MCUs or ASIC style execution and evaluated by latency, energy, or
memory footprint rather than accuracy alone [Bus22, Bus24, Yao22, Asa25, Jun24].

Two algorithmic references are especially useful for the report. Longformer replaces dense attention with
local windows plus task specific global tokens, providing a clean example of how linear scaling can be
achieved while keeping a path for long range information flow [Bel20]. Swin Transformer shows how non
overlapping local windows can be made more expressive by shifting the window partition across layers,
yielding hierarchical multi scale processing with linear complexity in image size [Liu21b]. EEG papers do
not copy these designs literally, but several MI models clearly inherit the same logic of local windows,
hierarchy, and cross window fusion [Wan23, Mou24, Qam26].

What the EEG MI literature actually uses

The most on target MI studies in this project rely on local temporal windows rather than truly global linear
attention. Hierarchical Window Attention for Motor Imagery EEG Classification builds token sequences
from temporal spatial embeddings, applies local window attention inside non overlapping temporal
windows, and stacks these windows hierarchically across scales. The paper argues that discriminative MI
information often resides in local temporal neighborhoods and reports strong performance on BCI III 3a
and BCI IV 2a [Mou24]. This is one of the clearest examples of a local attention design whose efficiency
story is aligned with EEG physiology.

The Swin inspired MI line reaches a similar conclusion by another route. The channel attention plus Swin
Transformer model uses channel weighting together with Swin style local windows to capture temporal
spectral spatial structure in MI data [Wan23]. Newer follow on work in the library extends this direction
toward subject independent decoding with compact convolutional Swin backbones and reduced FLOPs
[Qam26]. These papers are methodologically valuable because they make the bridge from computer vision
window attention to EEG especially explicit.

Sliding window models are the other strong branch. CSANet combines spatiotemporal convolution,
sliding windows, and local plus global attention stages, with gains on both within subject and between
subject MI tasks [Hua23b]. MCL SWT pushes the same idea toward subject independent recognition, using
a temporal sliding window Transformer together with mirror contrastive learning to sharpen lateralized
motor patterns [Luo24]. These models support a practical conclusion: in MI EEG, local windows are not
just a computational trick. They also act as a useful inductive bias for event related desynchronization
patterns that unfold over short sensorimotor intervals.

Hierarchical aggregation appears even when papers do not explicitly use Swin style windows. The
Hierarchical Transformer for MI BCI splits long trials into shorter intervals, uses a low level Transformer
within intervals, and then applies a high level Transformer across interval level features [Den23]. This

does not fully eliminate quadratic attention inside each block, but it reduces the cost of modeling long
trials by moving attention to a multi stage structure. It is therefore best understood as a near neighbor to
windowed attention rather than a separate family.

Compact backbones matter as much as attention type

A striking pattern in the library is that many of the most useful EEG models achieve efficiency less by
inventing a brand new linear attention rule and more by shrinking the token budget before attention. EEG
Conformer is the central example. It uses convolution to learn low level temporal and spatial features,
then passes these compact features to a Transformer block that captures wider correlations [Son22]. The
design is not linear in the strict theoretical sense, but it is compact, widely adopted, and strong enough
to become the baseline that later efficient models try to match or beat.

Compact convolutional transformer variants continue this trend. EEGCCT improves subject independent
MI decoding while using fewer parameters than several competing Transformer baselines [Keu24].
TinyEEGConformer takes the next step toward deployment, reporting 21 times fewer parameters than
EEG Conformer on BCI IV 2a while slightly improving accuracy and fitting within a small embedded
memory budget on Arm Cortex M processors [Asa25]. The lesson is that for current BCI practice, compact
front ends, shallow attention blocks, and careful parameter budgeting are often more decisive than a pure
change from dense to linear attention.

What counts as real time evidence

The MI literature is rich in efficiency intent but still thin in hard latency reporting. Many papers claim
suitability for real time BCI because they use local windows, fewer parameters, or lower FLOPs [Mou24,
Wan23, Keu24, Qam26]. Far fewer papers actually measure latency on target hardware. The clearest edge
deployment evidence in the project comes from adjacent EEG tasks rather than MI itself.

This evidence base supports a cautious but favorable conclusion. Efficient Transformer style EEG
inference on edge hardware is already feasible, but the strongest proof comes from seizure and sleep
applications where deployment papers report latency and energy directly [Bus22, Bus24, Yao22]. MI BCI
papers are closer to proving architectural plausibility than end to end real time readiness. TinyEEGCon
former is one of the few MI centered papers that begins to close this gap [Asa25].

Main conclusions for a focused review

   · Most relevant mechanism for the report

     Local window attention and hierarchical window aggregation are the best supported design pattern
     for EEG MI in this library [Mou24, Wan23, Hua23b, Den23]. They align computational efficiency with
     the short time scale structure of MI EEG.

   · Best foundational references

     Longformer and Swin Transformer are the strongest algorithmic anchors for explaining how con
     strained attention reduces ( 2) cost to linear or near linear scaling under fixed local windows
     [Bel20, Liu21b]. Linformer and Fastformer are useful secondary references for low rank and additive
     routes to linear attention [Wan20, Wu21].

   · Best EEG MI backbone references

     EEG Conformer remains the key baseline because it established a compact convolution plus attention
     recipe that much of the later work refines [Son22]. EEGCCT and TinyEEGConformer show how that
     recipe can be pushed toward smaller models and subject independent or embedded settings [Keu24,
     Asa25].

   · Best real time deployment references

     EEGformer on MCU platforms provides the strongest measured evidence for low latency and low
     energy Transformer style EEG inference [Bus22, Bus24]. These papers are outside MI, but they are
     highly relevant to any argument about deployment realism.

   · Current limitation of the field

     The literature still over indexes on offline accuracy. Surveys in the project consistently note limited
     standardization, data scarcity, and sparse reporting of latency, energy, and acquisition to feedback
     delay [Abi23, Keu24b, Pfe25]. This means claims about real time BCI readiness should be framed
     carefully.

Taken together, these papers support a clear synthesis. For EEG and especially MI BCI, the path from
quadratic attention to practical real time inference is currently driven less by pure linear attention theory
and more by local windows, hierarchy, early token reduction, and hardware aware implementation. The
field is moving toward deployable efficient Transformers, but direct proof on edge MI systems remains
limited and should be treated as an active research frontier rather than a solved problem [Asa25, Bus24,
Pfe25].
